Project Focus: The Wishlist Wardrobe

Our project focuses on improving the online clothing shopping experience by addressing pain points related to **virtual try-on**, **product comparison**, and **wishlist organization**. We aim to reduce the guesswork associated with online purchases and lower the rate of returns.

Problem Statement

Online clothing shoppers struggle with confidence in **determining correct fit, quality, and style compatibility** of garments before purchase, leading to high return rates and cognitive fatigue from comparing products across multiple platforms.

Solution & Value Proposition

Wishlist Wardrobe provides an integrated solution for visual organization, comparison, and outfit planning. It helps users: **A)** confidently assess fit and style compatibility using virtual tools, **B)** consolidate and organize items from multiple retailers, and **C)** manage their budget and optimize purchases.

Initial Research Summary

Virtual Try-On Challenges

Existing VTO technology often has issues with photo realism, cloth movement, and texture representation, making shoppers rely more on reviews and self-developed workarounds for confidence.

Decision-Making Fatigue

The sheer volume of online apparel choices and the lack of standardization across size guides contribute to decision fatigue and high cart abandonment rates.

The Rise of Secondhand Shopping

The growing interest in secondhand consumption highlights a user base that is highly price-conscious and requires better tools for assessing quality and condition before purchase.

Milestone 2: Contextual Inquiry Report

Patterns and Key Findings

  • Time plays an important role in shopping behaviors. Users prefer online shopping for speed and convenience, but prefer in-store shopping for sizing and quality assurance when time is not a factor.
  • Shoppers consistently return to familiar brands and retailers to alleviate obstacles such as fit, style familiarity, and material quality.
  • Customer reviews are a primary and highly trusted source of information for determining the correct size and quality, especially when shopping for unfamiliar brands.
  • Shoppers also look for videos provided by the brand/platform as a reliable source of visualizing the outfit/item they are looking to purchase.

Pain Points

  • The biggest pain point for shoppers is determining the correct size when shopping unfamiliar brands.
  • Limited size availability increases frustration and often leads to disappointment.
  • Cognitive overload commonly occurs while comparing multiple products, completing looks, and remembering items from different sites.
  • The effort required to find and apply discounts often outweighs the perceived savings.
  • Wishlists/Favorites pages become too chaotic without a way to organize items or track outfits for different people (family/friends).

Inefficiencies & Workarounds

  • **Workaround**: Users open multiple tabs to build outfits across different sites (multi-tab shopping), which increases cognitive load.
  • **Workaround**: Users buy multiple sizes of the same item to guarantee fit, leading to higher returns and increased workload.
  • **Inefficiency**: Not utilizing discount codes and promotional deals ends up costing the consumer more.
  • **Workaround**: Shoppers heavily rely on user-submitted reviews/photos on unfamiliar items for fit, style, and quality.

Raw Field Notes & Observations

Key Observation:

Participant uses Snapchat's "cutout" tool to create digital stickers of garments saved from online stores (Fashion Nova, Pretty Little Thing). They then combine these stickers onto a photo to create a **makeshift virtual try-on/outfit** organization workflow. This process is done frequently to assess how different items could potentially look together before purchasing.

Demographics:

  • **Age/Occupation**: 24, Warehouse Associate
  • **Shopping Setting**: Online, Monthly
  • **Category**: Wardrobe Additions, Virtual Try-On

Pain Points:

  • No integrated tools within shopping apps for organizing outfit ideas.
  • Fragmentation of information across many platforms (wishlist, camera roll, Snapchat).
  • No clear indication of sizing accuracy within her custom workflow.

Key Observation:

Participant experiences **cognitive overload** from overwhelming search results and cluttered Favorites lists. They rely on **price filters** and **customer-submitted photos/reviews** to narrow down choices and confirm fit/quality, despite checking size guides. They use multiple browser tabs to compare the same product across different brands for price and style.

Demographics:

  • **Age/Occupation**: 28, Data Analyst
  • **Shopping Setting**: Both, more online, Intermittent/Need-Based
  • **Category**: Young Professional

Key Quotes:

  • "My saved list gets really cluttered."
  • "When I search 'black dress,' the results are overwhelming."
  • "I wish I could sort my favorites into categories, like 'work' or 'casual.'"
  • "The size guide isn't very reassuring; I still check reviews to confirm fit."

Key Observation:

Participant is an **event-driven** shopper (shopping for a vacation). They stick to known retailers (Macy's app). Despite scrolling through many options, they **did not use filters**. The biggest pain point observed was **limited size availability**, leading to frustration and increased time spent finding suitable items. They did not look for additional discounts at checkout.

Demographics:

  • **Age/Occupation**: 35-40, Management and Parent
  • **Shopping Setting**: Online and In-store, Intermittent
  • **Category**: Parent/Professional

Pain Points:

  • Finding both their desired style and size, which took considerable time (14-18 minutes per item type).
  • Limited size availability.
  • Sizing varies depending on the brand.

Key Observation:

Participant's shopping style is **browsing with no specific plan**, often realizing a missing wardrobe piece (e.g., "a good black cardigan") during the process. They use the **cart as a comparison tool** for fit, color, price, and shipping speed across multiple websites. They perform external **Google Searches** to compare options from different stores.

Demographics:

  • **Age/Occupation**: 29, Physician Assistant
  • **Shopping Setting**: In-Store, Need-Based/Quarterly
  • **Category**: Wardrobe Additions, Brand Comparison

Pain Points:

  • Sizing and unknown quality, especially on sites like Amazon, which requires heavy reliance on reviews.

Key Quote:

"I would add options from each site to the cart in multiple tabs and then compare the carts when complete."

Overall Reflection

Our contextual inquiries revealed that while online shopping provides convenience, it often leads to **cognitive overload and increased stress**, particularly with sizing and product comparison. We determined that both novice and experienced shoppers encounter similar challenges and pain points. These observations reinforce the need for **more personalized and adaptive shopping tools** and will provide guidance for our design solutions to improve and simplify the shopping experience for all users.
