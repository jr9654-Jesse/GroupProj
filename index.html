The Problem

Shopping, both virtually and in-store, takes time and can be mentally overloading. Currently, there is no centralized space that allows shoppers to collect clothing from different stores. Additionally, shoppers are unable to fully visualize outfits before buying which can lead to mismatched outfits and unnecessary purchases. This burden falls on the consumer to return unwanted items, in-store or through postal services, and waiting for reimbursement.

The Solution

Create a centralized virtual wardrobe app that allows users to have access and collect items from any online store.

Features:

  • Users have a virtual environment they can access to mix and match items they have selected as well as having AI recommendations to assist them completing outfits.
  • Shoppers will have an organized virtual hub that saves time, money, and mental fatigue all while making fashion more accessible.
  • Users would be able to track prices for price drops, sales, and restocks.

Project Focus

Topic

Wishlist Wardrobe: A virtual wardrobe to make personal styling, online shopping, and reselling more efficient, sustainable, and worth your money.

Problem Statement

Personal styling and online shopping are currently independent experiences and require a lot of time and mental energy. While the rise of artificial intelligence (AI) and augmented reality (AR) have contributed to tools that allow online shoppers to visualize the outfits virtually prior to purchasing [1], there is no commercially available platform that allows shoppers to:

  • Manage their existing wardrobe and styles, virtually
  • Collect and experiment with different outfit combinations from different brands online
  • Sell old garments and pieces to other buyers
  • See how they look on themselves through AR
  • Compare sizes, designs, quality and cost between brands
  • Place their order for the selected items.

Solution

Wishlist Wardrobe is a consolidated platform that combines personal styling and planning with efficient online shopping. Supported by emerging AI and AR tools, the goal is to provide one platform where customers can select from a wide range of clothing brands, mix-and-match pieces, visualize the outfit on themselves, save items for future purchases, and complete their online order.

Building on previous work in this area [2], Wishlist Wardrobe will use AI support to identify differences in sizing patterns between brands, recommend outfits based on the consumer’s need (professional, casual, formal, special event, etc.), and learn from previous trends to differentiate a potentially successful purchase from one that is likely to be returned.

Additionally, there exists a need for users to social and environmental need for users to resell their clothing items [3]. This can be alleviated by giving users the ability to resell their clothing online alongside new items allowing users to match similar styles for a lower price and in a environmentally conscious way.

The platform can also use a camera or recent picture to recommend clothing sizes and learn from consumers’ product reviews to suggest if/when to size up or size down.

Contextual Inquiry Report

Patterns and Key Findings

  • Time plays an important role in shopping behaviors. Users prefer online shopping for speed and convenience, especially when time is a factor. However, when there are no time constraints, in-store shopping is preferred for sizing and quality assurance.
  • For both online and in-store shopping, shoppers admit that they consistently return to familiar brands and retailers to alleviate obstacles such as fit, style familiarity, and material quality.
  • Customer reviews are trusted by shoppers as a primary source of information related to determining the correct size, and quality. When shopping for unfamiliar brands, customer reviews are even more heavily relied on.
  • Shoppers also look for videos provided by the brand/platform as a reliable source of visualizing the outfit/item they are looking to purchase.

Pain Points

  • The biggest pain point for shoppers is determining the correct size when shopping unfamiliar brands.
  • Limited size availability increases frustration and often leads to disappointment.
  • Cognitive overload commonly occurs while comparing multiple products, completing looks, and remembering items from different sites.
  • The effort required to find and apply discounts often outweighs the perceived savings.
  • Wishlists/Favorites pages become too chaotic without a way to organize items or track outfits for different people (family/friends).

Inefficiencies

  • Shopping clearance items or use price filters to help maintain budget.
  • The time and workload affiliated with obtaining coupons outweighs the savings advantages. Not utilizing discount codes and promotional deals ends up costing the consumer more.
  • Mult-tab shopping increases cognitive load and reduces efficiency.

Workarounds

  • Users open multiple tabs to build outfits across different sites (multi-tab shopping).
  • Users buy multiple sizes of the same item to guarantee fit, leading to higher returns and increased workload.
  • Shoppers heavily rely on user-submitted reviews/photos on unfamiliar items for fit, style, and quality.

Actionable Insights

  • Incorporate favorites or digital closet feature that allows users to save and categorize pieces.
    • Favorites can be customizable to allow them to create their own categories (tops, winter clothes, shoes, accessories, etc.)
  • Stylist can create favorites/digital closet for each client based on preferences, budget, and needs
  • Include custom filters for price, size, style, brand and customer reviews
  • Include the ability to attach notes to pieces to help track preferences or concerns
  • Implement a cross-site item comparison tool. Provides the ability to:
    • Compare items from different websites
    • Provide the user the ability to find items for the best price and fit
    • Compare similar items from various brands to assist with making the best styling selection
  • Incorporate digital stylist/shopping assistant that:
    • Provides a visual and realistic idea of what the completed outfit will look like with their current selection
    • A style swap option and suggestion to obtain the desired style
    • Incorporate recommendations based off users closet, past purchases, and current selections
    • Serves as a guide to complete shopping goals
  • Show customer reviews and brand media for each piece
    • Customer photos, sizing feedback, product quality
    • Videos, photos, and information from the brand, for example: “Model is 5’3” and wearing size M”

Challenges

Of the participants we interviewed, there was a split between shopping enthusiasts and people who shop for necessity rather than enjoyment. The former group struggled less with time and more with efficiency. As they are familiar with the platforms and spend a significant amount of time on them, their main concerns were not being able to keep track of the items they liked in the way that was most helpful to them, and not being sure about sizing and quality with new brands that did not have enough customer reviews or brand media. For the latter group,time pressures were a recurring issue which led to incomplete outfit purchases, overspending due to rushed decisions, and settling for items out of convenience rather than desired items.

Unexpected Findings

  • Online shopping did not lead to a reduction in fatigue, stress, and feeling overwhelmed despite the convenience.
  • Experienced stylists and casual shoppers often face the same challenges when shopping both online and in-store.
  • Although shoppers have access to coupons and discounts, shoppers were reluctant to use them due to the inconvenience of locating and applying them. The time and effort required outweighed their perceived savings.

Future Research Improvements

  • Specific Changes
    • The contextual inquiries were conducted independently by each member of the team. This allowed the team to have a larger sample size and collect data from a diverse sample. To improve our inquiries in the future, it would be beneficial to conduct a contextual inquiry session with all the team members observing the same participant. Multiple observers would be able to capture subtle behaviors, emotional reactions, and follow-up questions an individual observer may have missed.
    • Future rounds of testing would benefit from an increased focus on people who enjoy shopping and styling their outfits, especially online. As our proposed product aims to address gaps in online shopping for multi-brand items with size, price and quality comparisons, with virtual try-on options, focusing on a larger group of users who engage with these functionalities will help us refine the services we provide and the interaction design.
  • Additional Methods:
    • To further refine our contextual inquiries, we would increase our sample size and incorporate additional methods such as surveys and usability testing to collect additional data. Expanding our sample size would enable more diverse feedback from shoppers with varying experience levels, shopping habits, and brand preferences.
    • Surveys would allow us to collect additional quantitative data on frequency of pain points and qualitative feedback on potential solutions. These additional methods would help us validate our findings and reinforce our design decisions.

Overall Reflection

Our contextual inquiries revealed that while online shopping provides convenience, it often leads to cognitive overload and increased stress, particularly with sizing and product comparison. We determined that both novice and experienced shoppers encounter similar challenges and pain points. These observations reinforce the need for more personalized and adaptive shopping tools and will provide guidance for our design solutions to improve and simplify the shopping experience for all users.

Data Modeling: Personas and Hierarchical Task Analysis

Justification for Models

Our contextual inquiry observations revealed that users get frustrated at multiple stages in the process of online shopping. Depending on their needs and goals, they are shopping with different timelines, for themselves or others, across multiple websites when desired items are not available on one platform, and they experience challenges with assembling whole outfits that can be visualized before purchasing, and tracked separately depending on whom they are intended for. Returning items is also a concern and a time-consuming task.

The collection of these observations and concerns led us to select a task analysis model, specifically a hierarchical task analysis to define the series of steps users follow while shopping online. From searching for desired items to completing transactions and managing orders/returns, the hierarchical task analysis allows us to visualize the complete user experience and design our platform accordingly. We also include considerations for cognitive load and their impact on our design to demonstrate how our platform aims to address issues discovered during contextual inquiry observations, represented by our personas.

Context

Hailey is a full-time software engineer and parent. Work deadlines and parenting responsibilities limit her ability to browse multiple stores in person. While online shopping is convenient, she is frustrated by the time-consuming and disorganized nature of managing multiple tabs, brand comparison, and sizing guides. She needs a solution to save time, stay organized, and confidently shop for herself and family without the stress of sizing.

Goals

  • Easily compare prices across multiple sites and brands.
  • Shop for both herself and family on a single platform.
  • Find discounts and best prices automatically.
  • Buy from any brand without worrying about proper fit or quality.
Pain Points
  • Cognitive overload from managing multiple tabs and sites for comparison.
  • Inconsistent sizing between brands, leading to frustration and increased workload from returns.
  • Inability to categorize favorites for herself and family members.

Hierarchical Task Analysis (HTA)

Considerations for Cognitive Load and Impact on Design

  • Decision Fatigue: Our platform will facilitate swapping items between outfits and visualizing outfits as a whole to inform decision-making, addressing the manual tracking of outfit combinations.
  • Reliance on Memory: Our platform will provide a consolidated page to view all selected items, organized by themes, shoppers, and other customizable categories, eliminating the need for users to remember whom each item was intended for.
  • Efficiency: Our platform will allow adding all items in a designed outfit to the cart to improve efficiency and package desired items together for personalized delivery, unlike current systems that only allow adding individual items.
  • Error Prevention: Our platform will include real-time stock updates and notifications about shipping rates before adding to the cart to create a more seamless user experience, addressing frustration from finding out about out-of-stock items or shipping charges at the final step.
